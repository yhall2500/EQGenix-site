Origin

EQGenix did not begin as a business model. It began as a lived observation, refined over decades of work with populations that most institutions have failed, at-risk youth, blended families navigating impossible dynamics, veterans translating military discipline into civilian uncertainty, and professionals confronting the limits of their own cognitive architecture.

The common thread was always the same: the people who thrived were not the ones with the most resources, the best connections, or the highest intelligence. They were the ones who had built the cognitive and emotional infrastructure to sustain themselves under pressure, to regulate without numbing, to connect without losing sovereignty, and to endure when the motivation had long since expired.

Methodology

This infrastructure is not innate. It is not downloadable. It is built. And the methodology for building it is what EQGenix exists to provide.

As the founder of EQGenix Inc. and the director of Life in Darkness, a 501(c)(3) that licenses EQGenix frameworks for underserved communities, I have spent my career developing the curricula, assessment tools, and formation methodologies that translate neuroscience principles into practical, repeatable systems for human capacity development.

Formation

My work is informed by formal training in technology, social and political science, and psychology, and by the irreplaceable education of raising seven children, caring for twelve grandchildren, twenty years of caregiving for my mother, and serving as a Guardian ad Litem in Florida and California courts.

The Emotional Portfolio™ framework is the synthesis of everything I have learned: that emotional wealth compounds, that cognitive sovereignty must be trained, that resilience is architecture not attitude, and that the human capacities AI cannot replicate are precisely the ones we have most neglected to develop.
